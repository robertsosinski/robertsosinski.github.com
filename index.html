About Robert Sosinski

Robert is a digital transformation executive with the proven history of leading complex organizations through ambitious business and technology challenges. As a Presidential Innovation Fellow at Barack Obama's White House, Robert reformed digital services across the Federal Government with his expertise in cutting-edge technology and effective software delivery.

Robert was also a senior member of Amazon's acquisition landing team for the $14 billion purchase of Whole Foods. There, Robert advised executive leadership across Amazon and Whole Foods on migrating mission-critical systems onto AWS to launch Prime Now Delivery, Prime Membership Rewards at Retail Checkout, and Whole Foods products on Amazon.com.

Robert also founded Reactive.IO, a consultancy which helps businesses of all sizes build their mission-critical software, faster. There, Robert advised enterprise customers such as Goldman Sachs, Bloomberg, Citi Group, and the National Football League on leveraging cloud computing to effectively scale globally available services and launch real-time big-data analytics systems.

Before that, Robert led engineering teams at Sling Media (acquired by Dish Network), Time Inc., Health.com, Entertainment Weekly, The University of Pennsylvania, and Switch2Health (acquired by FitBit). Robert also served in the 70th Intelligence Wing of the U.S. Air Force as a Cryptographic Specialist while stationed in Tokyo, Japan.

Robert holds an Executive Masters in Public Leadership from The University of Texas, a M.S. in Management of Technology from New York University, and a B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Maryland.

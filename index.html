About Robert Sosinski

Robert leads large and complex firms through mission-critical digital transformations with a focus on Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Cloud Computing. As a Presidential Innovation Fellow at Barack Obama's White House, Robert reformed digital services across the Federal Government so millions of veterans could get faster access to healthcare and benefits.

Robert was also a senior member of Amazon's acquisition landing team for the $14 billion purchase of Whole Foods. There, Robert advised executive leadership across Amazon and Whole Foods on effectively migrating mission-critical systems onto AWS to launch Prime Now Delivery, Prime Membership Rewards at Checkout, and Whole Foods Groceries on Amazon.com.

Robert also founded Reactive.IO, a consultancy which helps businesses of all sizes build their mission-critical software, faster. There, Robert advised enterprise customers such as Bloomberg, Goldman Sachs, and the National Football League on leveraging cloud computing and machine learning to effectively launch and scale globally available real-time financial analysis systems.

Previously, Robert was the VP of Engineering at Ticket Evolution (acquired by Victory Live), where he led the development of advanced software products that serve as the backbone of the event industryâ€™s multi-billion dollar ticket market. Before that, Robert led cloud infrastructure engineering at Sling (acquired by Dish Network), where he leveraged elastic compute and edge networks to build globally available systems to encoded and serve hundreds of terabytes of HD video for live and on-demand streaming.

Before that, Robert engineered highly scalable web applications for Time Inc., The University of Pennsylvania, and Switch2Health (acquired by Fitbit). Robert also served in the 70th Intelligence Wing of the U.S. Air Force as a Cryptographic Specialist while stationed in Tokyo, Japan.

Robert holds an Executive Masters in Public Leadership from The University of Texas, a M.S. in Management of Technology from New York University, and a B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Maryland.
